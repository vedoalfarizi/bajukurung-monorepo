# Requirements Document

## Introduction

This document defines the requirements for an MVP e-commerce platform selling Malaysian Baju Kurung (traditional Malay attire) to Indonesian customers. The platform supports a pre-order workflow where customers can place orders before items are produced or shipped, occasion-based product browsing (Raya/Eid, Wedding, Casual), a standardised sizing system, and a scalable serverless architecture on AWS.

The MVP targets Indonesian buyers who want access to authentic Malaysian Baju Kurung, with a seamless shopping experience in Bahasa Indonesia, pricing in Indonesian Rupiah (IDR), and cross-border fulfilment from Malaysia. No customer registration or login is required — customers complete their order by sending a pre-filled WhatsApp message to the Seller. Payment is handled manually by the Seller outside the Platform.

---

## Glossary

- **Platform**: The AWS-based e-commerce web application described in this document.
- **Customer**: An Indonesian end-user who browses products and places orders via WhatsApp on the Platform.
- **Seller**: The Malaysian business owner who lists products, receives WhatsApp orders, and fulfils them.
- **Product**: A Baju Kurung item listed on the Platform, described by occasion, size, colour, and fabric.
- **Pre-Order**: An order placed by a Customer for a Product that has not yet been produced or is not yet in stock.
- **Pre-Order Window**: The defined period during which Customers may place Pre-Orders for a specific Product, bounded by a start date and an end date.
- **Occasion**: A category grouping Products by intended use — one of: Raya (Eid), Wedding, or Casual.
- **Standard Size**: A size designation from the Platform's standardised sizing chart (XS, S, M, L, XL, XXL, AllSize), mapped to body measurements in centimetres.
- **AllSize**: A special size label indicating the garment is designed to fit a range of body types, typically equivalent to M or L. Products labelled AllSize do not require the Customer to select a specific size.
- **Cart**: A temporary collection of Products selected by a Customer before generating a WhatsApp order message.
- **WhatsApp Order Message**: A pre-filled message generated by the Platform summarising the Customer's Cart, intended to be sent by the Customer to the Seller via WhatsApp.
- **Order**: A purchase record created automatically by the Platform when a Customer completes checkout, with status "Pending Confirmation" until the Seller confirms it.
- **Customer WhatsApp Number**: The Indonesian WhatsApp number provided by the Customer at checkout, stored with the Order to allow the Seller to follow up if the Customer does not send the WhatsApp message.
- **Order Status**: The current state of an Order — one of: `PENDING`, `PAYMENT_PENDING`, `PACKAGED`, `READY_TO_SHIP`, `SHIPPED`, `DELIVERED`, `CANCELLED`, `REFUND`.
- **Order Status Transition**: A seller-initiated change from one Order Status to the next valid state, which may require additional data (e.g. tracking link, proof photos, refund amount).
- **WhatsApp Copyable Message**: A pre-formatted text string generated by the Platform and displayed in the Seller dashboard, ready to be copied and pasted into WhatsApp to communicate with the Customer.
- **Catalogue**: The collection of Products currently open for Pre-Order on the Platform.
- **API**: The AWS API Gateway-backed REST interface used by the frontend.
- **Product Service**: The backend service responsible for managing the Catalogue, including product creation by the Seller.
- **Product Size Chart**: A per-product mapping of available Standard Sizes to their specific body measurement ranges (bust, waist, hip in cm), defined by the Seller when creating a product.
- **Order Service**: The backend service responsible for managing Orders.
- **IDR**: Indonesian Rupiah, the currency used for all pricing on the Platform.

## Requirements

### Requirement 0: Product Management for Seller

**User Story:** As a Seller, I want to add new products via the dashboard, so that I can list Baju Kurung items for customers to browse and pre-order.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Platform SHALL provide a product creation form on the Seller dashboard accessible only to authenticated Seller accounts.
2. WHEN a Seller creates a product, THE Platform SHALL require the following fields: name, occasion, description, fabric type, colours, available sizes, price in IDR, at least one product image, Pre-Order Window start date, and Pre-Order Window end date.
3. THE Seller SHALL be able to upload one or more product images when creating a product, and THE Platform SHALL store the images in Amazon S3.
4. WHEN a Seller creates a product, THE Seller SHALL be able to define a size chart for that product by specifying bust (cm), waist (cm), and hip (cm) measurement ranges for each available Standard Size.
5. WHEN a Seller submits a product creation form with missing required fields, THE Platform SHALL display validation errors and SHALL NOT create the product.
6. WHEN a product is successfully created, THE Product Service SHALL make it immediately available in the Catalogue if the current date falls within the Pre-Order Window.

---

**User Story:** As a Customer, I want to browse Baju Kurung by occasion (Raya, Wedding, Casual), so that I can quickly find products suited to my needs.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Product Service SHALL categorise every Product under exactly one Occasion: Raya, Wedding, or Casual.
2. THE Catalogue SHALL only display Products whose Pre-Order Window is currently open (i.e., the current date is on or after the Pre-Order Window start date and on or before the Pre-Order Window end date).
3. WHEN a Customer selects an Occasion, THE Platform SHALL display only Products belonging to that Occasion that are currently open for Pre-Order.
4. WHEN a Customer views the Catalogue, THE Platform SHALL display each Product with its name, primary image, price in IDR, available Standard Sizes, Pre-Order Window start date, and Pre-Order Window end date.
5. WHEN a Customer applies a size filter, THE Platform SHALL display only Products available in the selected Standard Size.
6. WHEN a Customer views a Product detail page, THE Platform SHALL display the product name, all images, full description, fabric type, available colours, Standard Size chart with body measurements in centimetres, price in IDR, Pre-Order Window start date, and Pre-Order Window end date.
7. WHEN the Catalogue contains no Products matching the selected filters, THE Platform SHALL display a message indicating no results were found.

### Requirement 2: Standardised Sizing System

**User Story:** As a Customer, I want to select my size using a standardised size chart, so that I can order confidently without needing custom measurements.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Platform SHALL support exactly seven Standard Sizes: XS, S, M, L, XL, XXL, and AllSize.
2. EACH Product SHALL store its own size chart — a mapping of each available Standard Size to a defined range of body measurements: bust (cm), waist (cm), and hip (cm) — set by the Seller at product creation time.
3. WHEN a Customer views the size chart on a Product detail page, THE Platform SHALL display the bust, waist, and hip measurement ranges in centimetres specific to that product for each available Standard Size, and SHALL display a descriptive note for AllSize indicating it fits a range of body types equivalent to M or L.
4. WHEN a Customer selects a Standard Size for a Product, THE Platform SHALL only allow selection of sizes that are available for that specific Product.
5. IF a Product is listed with only AllSize available, THEN THE Platform SHALL automatically select AllSize and SHALL NOT require the Customer to choose a size.
6. IF a Customer attempts to add a Product to the Cart without selecting a Standard Size (for Products that offer multiple sizes), THEN THE Platform SHALL display an error message prompting size selection and SHALL NOT add the item to the Cart.

---

### Requirement 3: Pre-Order Workflow

**User Story:** As a Customer, I want to place a Pre-Order for a Baju Kurung before it is produced, so that I can secure my preferred design and size ahead of availability.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Seller SHALL be able to create a Product listing designated as a Pre-Order with a defined Pre-Order Window start date and end date.
2. WHILE a Pre-Order Window is open, THE Platform SHALL allow Customers to add the designated Product to their Cart.
3. IF a Customer attempts to add a Pre-Order Product to the Cart after the Pre-Order Window has closed, THEN THE Platform SHALL display a message indicating the Pre-Order period has ended and SHALL NOT add the item to the Cart.
4. WHEN a Customer initiates checkout, THE Order Service SHALL create an Order record with status `PENDING` containing the Customer's name, Customer WhatsApp number, cart line items, and total price in IDR, and SHALL return an Order ID to the Platform.
5. THE valid Order Status transitions are:
   - `PENDING` → `PAYMENT_PENDING` (Seller finalises order)
   - `PAYMENT_PENDING` → `PACKAGED` (Seller uploads proof of payment)
   - `PACKAGED` → `READY_TO_SHIP` (Seller marks ready for logistics)
   - `READY_TO_SHIP` → `SHIPPED` (Seller fills in tracking link)
   - `SHIPPED` → `DELIVERED` (Seller confirms delivery, may upload proof photo)
   - `PENDING` → `CANCELLED` (Seller cancels on customer's behalf)
   - `PAYMENT_PENDING` → `CANCELLED`
   - `PACKAGED` → `REFUND` (Seller processes refund with amount and proof photo)
   - `SHIPPED` → `REFUND`
   - `DELIVERED` → `REFUND`
6. WHEN a Seller updates an Order status, THE Order Service SHALL record the status change with a timestamp.

### Requirement 4: Shopping Cart and WhatsApp Checkout

**User Story:** As a Customer, I want to add products to a cart and generate a WhatsApp message, so that I can send my order details to the Seller without needing to register or log in.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a Customer adds a Product to the Cart, THE Platform SHALL store the selected Product, Standard Size, quantity, and unit price in IDR.
2. WHEN a Customer views the Cart, THE Platform SHALL display each line item with product name, selected Standard Size, quantity, unit price in IDR, and a calculated line total in IDR.
3. THE Platform SHALL display the Cart subtotal in IDR as the sum of all line item totals.
4. WHEN a Customer removes an item from the Cart, THE Platform SHALL update the Cart total accordingly.
5. WHEN a Customer initiates checkout, THE Platform SHALL call the Order Service to create an Order, then generate a WhatsApp Order Message containing the Order ID, the Customer's name (entered at checkout), each line item with product name, Standard Size, quantity, and unit price in IDR, the Cart subtotal in IDR, and the Seller's WhatsApp number as the recipient.
6. WHEN the WhatsApp Order Message is generated, THE Platform SHALL open the message in WhatsApp (via the WhatsApp deep link) so the Customer can review and send it directly to the Seller.
7. THE Platform SHALL NOT require the Customer to create an account or log in at any point during browsing or checkout.
8. IF the Cart is empty when a Customer initiates checkout, THEN THE Platform SHALL display an error message and SHALL NOT generate a WhatsApp Order Message.
9. WHEN a Customer initiates checkout, THE Platform SHALL require the Customer to enter their WhatsApp number before creating an Order.
10. THE Order Service SHALL store the Customer's WhatsApp number with the Order so the Seller can follow up with Customers who have not sent the WhatsApp message.

---

### Requirement 5: Order Management for Seller

**User Story:** As a Seller, I want to manage incoming orders and update their statuses, so that I can coordinate production, payment, and fulfilment efficiently.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Platform SHALL provide a Seller dashboard accessible only to authenticated Seller accounts.
2. WHEN a Seller views the dashboard, THE Platform SHALL display all Orders with their Order Status, Customer name, Customer WhatsApp number, line items, total price in IDR, and order date.
3. THE Seller SHALL be able to filter Orders by Order Status on the dashboard.
4. WHEN a Seller transitions an Order to `PAYMENT_PENDING`, THE Seller SHALL be able to update or finalise the order details (line items, quantities, total price), and THE Platform SHALL generate a WhatsApp Copyable Message containing the finalised order summary for the Seller to send to the Customer.
5. WHEN a Seller transitions an Order to `PACKAGED`, THE Seller SHALL be required to upload a photo as proof of payment received.
6. WHEN a Seller transitions an Order to `SHIPPED`, THE Seller SHALL be required to enter a tracking link, and THE Platform SHALL generate a WhatsApp Copyable Message containing the tracking link for the Seller to send to the Customer.
7. WHEN a Seller transitions an Order to `DELIVERED`, THE Seller MAY upload a photo as proof of receipt of the package.
8. WHEN a Seller transitions an Order to `REFUND`, THE Seller SHALL be required to enter the refund amount in IDR and upload a photo as proof of refund.
9. WHEN a Seller updates an Order status, THE Order Service SHALL record the status change with a timestamp.
10. IF a Seller attempts an invalid status transition, THE Platform SHALL display an error and SHALL NOT update the Order status.
11. WHEN a Seller views an Order, THE Platform SHALL display the Order Status, Customer name, Customer WhatsApp number, line items, total price in IDR, order date, tracking link (if available), proof photos (if available), and refund amount (if applicable).

---

### Requirement 6: Serverless AWS Architecture

**User Story:** As the Seller, I want the Platform to run on a scalable serverless AWS architecture, so that infrastructure costs scale with demand and the system handles traffic spikes during Raya season.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Platform SHALL deploy all backend compute using AWS Lambda functions.
2. THE Platform SHALL use Amazon API Gateway to expose all backend API endpoints.
3. THE Platform SHALL use Amazon DynamoDB as the primary data store for Products and Orders.
4. THE Platform SHALL use Amazon S3 to store and serve Product images.
5. THE Platform SHALL use Amazon CloudFront to serve the frontend application and Product images with low latency to Indonesian customers.
6. THE Platform SHALL use Amazon Cognito exclusively for Seller authentication and authorisation on the Seller dashboard.
7. WHEN a Lambda function invocation fails due to an unhandled error, THE Platform SHALL log the error to Amazon CloudWatch Logs.
8. THE Platform SHALL use AWS IAM roles with least-privilege permissions for all Lambda functions and services.
9. WHERE the Platform is deployed to a production environment, THE Platform SHALL use separate AWS environments (accounts or namespaces) for staging and production.
